Our Story

A coffee shop and a tea house, under one roof in La Crosse

The Short Version

Two small La Crosse businesses, one small La Crosse save.

Global Grounds opened on State Street in 2010. Thippi Luangpraseuth opened her Dim Sum Tea Shop downtown at 221 Pearl Street a few years later, built around an 80 plus loose leaf tea wall and a deep love of boba.

In 2020 the pandemic hit both businesses hard. In April 2021 Thippi took over Global Grounds and folded Dim Sum into the shop on State Street. Coffee on one side, the tea wall on the other, and a room full of plants between them.

Alina Piotrowski, who owned Global Grounds from 2019 to 2021, stayed on as General Manager through the transition and still runs the floor.

“The pandemic really hit small businesses hard and in order to keep going, we had to think creatively.”
Thippi Luangpraseuth, Owner

What Makes Us, Us

A handful of things we will not compromise on.

01

Local Beans

Every cup of coffee is pulled on Bean Juice Coffee beans, roasted a mile up the road in small batches. Shorter supply chain, fresher espresso.

02

The Tea Wall

80 plus loose leaf teas carried over from Dim Sum Tea Shop. Black, green, herbal, boba, and rotating specialties. The staff can guide you through.

03

Plants and Art

Hanging plants live everywhere. Cultural art on the walls. The room changes a little every week because Thippi cannot leave well enough alone.

04

Sit a While

We are a sit-in cafe, not a grab-and-go. Bring a book, pull up a leather chair, use the free Wi-Fi, stay as long as your cup lasts.

05

Real Fruit Smoothies

Frozen fruit, whole milk or oat, nothing from a powder. Smoothie bowls topped with granola and whatever berries came in this week.

06

Reusable Mugs

Bring your own mug and take a small discount. Less trash out the door, more money in your pocket.
